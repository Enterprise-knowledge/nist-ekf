# Summary

Legal and regulatory requirements involving AI are understood, managed, and documented.

# Subcategories

- [GOVERN 1.1](../subcategories/govern-1-1.md) - Legal and regulatory requirements involving AI are understood, managed, and documented.
- [GOVERN 1.2](../subcategories/govern-1-2.md) - The characteristics of trustworthy AI are integrated into organizational policies, processes, and procedures.
- [GOVERN 1.3](../subcategories/govern-1-3.md) - Processes and procedures are in place to determine the needed level of risk management activities based on the organization's risk tolerance.
- [GOVERN 1.4](../subcategories/govern-1-4.md) - The risk management process and its outcomes are established through transparent policies, procedures, and other controls based on organizational risk priorities.
- [GOVERN 1.5](../subcategories/govern-1-5.md) - Ongoing monitoring and periodic review of the risk management process and its outcomes are planned, organizational roles and responsibilities are clearly defined, including determining the frequency of periodic review.
- [GOVERN 1.6](../subcategories/govern-1-6.md) - Mechanisms are in place to inventory AI systems and are resourced according to organizational risk priorities.
- [GOVERN 1.7](../subcategories/govern-1-7.md) - Processes and procedures are in place for decommissioning and phasing out of AI systems safely and in a manner that does not increase risks or decrease the organization’s trustworthiness.
