# Summary

The organization's priorities, constraints, risk tolerance and appetite statements, and assumptions are established, communicated, and used to support operational risk decisions

# Structure

This Category is part of `GV` and contains 7 active Subcategories.

## Subcategories

- [GV.RM-01](../subcategories/gv.rm-01.md) - Risk management objectives are established and agreed to by organizational stakeholders
- [GV.RM-02](../subcategories/gv.rm-02.md) - Risk appetite and risk tolerance statements are established, communicated, and maintained
- [GV.RM-03](../subcategories/gv.rm-03.md) - Cybersecurity risk management activities and outcomes are included in enterprise risk management processes
- [GV.RM-04](../subcategories/gv.rm-04.md) - Strategic direction that describes appropriate risk response options is established and communicated
- [GV.RM-05](../subcategories/gv.rm-05.md) - Lines of communication across the organization are established for cybersecurity risks, including risks from suppliers and other third parties
- [GV.RM-06](../subcategories/gv.rm-06.md) - A standardized method for calculating, documenting, categorizing, and prioritizing cybersecurity risks is established and communicated
- [GV.RM-07](../subcategories/gv.rm-07.md) - Strategic opportunities (i.e., positive risks) are characterized and are included in organizational cybersecurity risk discussions
